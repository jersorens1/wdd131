What are UTVs?

Utility Task Vehicles (UTVs), also known as side-by-sides, are versatile, off-road vehicles designed for a variety of work and recreational activities. They are similar to ATVs (All-Terrain Vehicles) but typically larger and can accommodate multiple passengers.

Features and Design

UTVs usually have side-by-side seating for two to six passengers, hence the nickname "side-by-sides." They often come with seat belts and roll cages for enhanced safety. UTVs are powered by gasoline or diesel engines, and electric models are also available. They typically feature automatic transmissions, though manual options exist. Most UTVs come with a rear cargo bed, similar to a small pickup truck, which can be used to carry tools, equipment, and other loads. Some models have the capability to tow trailers, further enhancing their utility. Many UTVs offer selectable 2WD, 4WD, and even 4WD with differential lock to handle various terrains. They feature robust suspension systems and large, rugged tires designed for off-road conditions.
